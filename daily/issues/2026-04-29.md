### [Contracting Tensor Networks with Generalized Belief Propagation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24760v1)
**Authors:** Joseph Tindall, Grace M. Sommers, Hilbert Kappen
**Published:** 2026-04-27
**Summary:** Generalized belief propagation (GBP) improves tensor network contraction accuracy by passing messages within overlapping regions.

**What's new**
- GBP extends standard belief propagation to hierarchical overlapping regions for better accuracy.
- Implemented on 2D/3D infinite and finite tensor networks with numerical and analytical fixed-point solutions.
- Applied to frustrated Ising model, 3D ice models, deformed AKLT state, and random tensor networks.

### [Experimental high-dimensional multi-qubit Bell non-locality on a superconducting quantum processor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24740v1)
**Authors:** Yousef Mafi, Ali G. Moghaddam, Teemu Ojanen
**Published:** 2026-04-27
**Summary:** Experimentally observed high-dimensional Bell non-locality using 12 qubits on a superconducting processor.

**What's new**
- Demonstrated Bell violation in two d=64-dimensional systems encoded in twelve qubits.
- Observed nonlocal correlations exceeding the quantum upper bound for d=2 systems up to d=32.
- Showed that the violation is genuinely collective, with all qubits contributing while pairwise correlations remain Bell-local.

### [The Optimal Sample Complexity of Multiclass and List Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24749v1)
**Authors:** Chirag Pabbaraju
**Published:** 2026-04-27
**Summary:** This paper resolves the optimal sample complexity for multiclass and list learning by proving the DS dimension bounds hypergraph density.

**What's new**
- Proves that maximum hypergraph density of a multiclass hypothesis class is bounded by its DS dimension.
- Confirms a longstanding conjecture by Daniely and Shalev-Shwartz (2014).
- Determines optimal sample complexity dependence on DS dimension for multiclass and list learning.
