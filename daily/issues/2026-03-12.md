### [Think Before You Lie: How Reasoning Improves Honesty](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09957v1)
**Authors:** Ann Yuan, Asma Ghandeharioun, Carter Blum, Alicia Machado, Jessica Hoffmann, Daphne Ippolito, Martin Wattenberg, Lucas Dixon, Katja Filippova
**Published:** 2026-03-10
**Summary:** LLMs become more honest when they reason, unlike humans, due to the metastable nature of deceptive regions in their representational space.

**What's new**
- Shows reasoning increases honesty in LLMs across scales and families, opposite to human behavior where deliberation reduces honesty.
- Introduces a dataset of realistic moral trade-offs where honesty incurs variable costs to study deceptive behavior.
- Finds reasoning traces are poor predictors of final behavior; the effect is linked to representational space geometry.
- Reveals deceptive regions are metastable, more easily destabilized by paraphrasing, resampling, or noise than honest ones.
- Interprets reasoning as traversing a biased space, nudging models toward stable, honest defaults.

### [From Data Statistics to Feature Geometry: How Correlations Shape Superposition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09972v1)
**Authors:** Lucas Prieto, Edward Stevinson, Melih Barsbey, Tolga Birdal, Pedro A. M. Mediano
**Published:** 2026-03-10
**Summary:** The paper shows that in realistic data with correlated features, superposition can use interference constructively to form semantic clusters, not just as noise to filter out.

**What's new**
- Introduces Bag-of-Words Superposition (BOWS) to study superposition with correlated features from internet text.
- Finds interference between correlated features can be constructive, not just noise, by aligning with co-activation patterns.
- Shows such arrangements arise with weight decay and naturally form semantic clusters and cyclical structures.
- Challenges the standard view of superposition as solely minimizing interference in sparse, uncorrelated settings.

### [OptEMA: Adaptive Exponential Moving Average for Stochastic Optimization with Zero-Noise Optimality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09923v1)
**Authors:** Ganzhao Yuan
**Published:** 2026-03-10
**Summary:** OptEMA introduces adaptive EMA variants for stochastic optimization that achieve near-optimal rates in both noisy and zero-noise regimes without requiring Lipschitz constants or manual hyperparameter retuning.

**What's new**
- Achieves noise-adaptive convergence rate O~(T^{-1/2}+σ^{1/2}T^{-1/4}) for average gradient norm
- Reduces to near-optimal deterministic rate O~(T^{-1/2}) in zero-noise regime (σ=0)

- Overcomes limitations of prior Adam analyses: suboptimal zero-noise guarantees and restrictive boundedness assumptions
