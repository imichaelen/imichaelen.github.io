## Featured Papers

### [MARS: Enabling Autoregressive Models Multi-Token Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07023v1)
**Authors:** Ziqi Jin, Lei Wang, Ziwei Luo, Aixin Sun
**Published:** 2026-04-08
**Summary:** MARS is a fine-tuning method that enables autoregressive language models to predict multiple tokens per forward pass, boosting inference speed without architectural changes or extra parameters.

**What's new**
- Enables multi-token generation via lightweight fine-tuning on existing instruction data, no architectural changes or extra parameters needed
- Maintains baseline accuracy while achieving 1.5-1.7x throughput when accepting multiple tokens per step
- Introduces block-level KV caching for batch inference, achieving up to 1.71x wall-clock speedup
- Supports real-time speed adjustment via confidence thresholding, providing a latency-quality knob for deployment

### [ReDAct: Uncertainty-Aware Deferral for LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07036v1)
**Authors:** Dzianis Piatrashyn, Nikita Kotelevskii, Kirill Grishchenkov, Nikita Glazkov, Ivan Nasonov, Ilya Makarov, Timothy Baldwin, Preslav Nakov, Roman Vashurin, Maxim Panov
**Published:** 2026-04-08
**Summary:** ReDAct is a method for LLM agents that defers uncertain decisions from a small, cheap model to a large, reliable model, cutting costs while maintaining performance.

**What's new**
- Proposes ReDAct, an uncertainty-aware deferral system using two LLMs: a small default model and a large fallback model.
- Defers decisions to the large model only when the small model's uncertainty exceeds a calibrated threshold.
- Achieves performance matching exclusive use of the large model while deferring only about 15% of decisions.
- Demonstrates effectiveness in text-based embodied environments like ALFWorld and MiniGrid.

### [Topological Magneto-Optical Switching in Even-Layered MnBi$_2$Te$_4$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07315v1)
**Authors:** Shahid Sattar, Roman Stepanov, C. M. Canali
**Published:** 2026-04-08
**Summary:** Even-layered MnBi2Te4 films enable topological magneto-optical switching via surface spin control, yielding tunable Chern numbers and quantized Faraday rotation.

**What's new**
- Outermost layer spin alignment controls Chern number and magneto-optical response, independent of PT-symmetry and net magnetization.
- Switching surface spins in a 6-layer film changes it from a C=0 axion insulator to a C=1 Chern insulator with quantized response.
- Thicker 12-layer films support a higher Chern number C=2 phase, doubling the low-frequency Faraday rotation.
- Magneto-optical spectroscopy is established as a direct probe for surface magnetism and topological order in these films.

### [Atomic-Scale Detection of Néel Vector Switching in the Single-Layer A-type Antiferromagnet Cr2S3-2D](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07245v1)
**Authors:** Affan Safeer, Calisa Dias, Mahdi Ghorbani-Asl, Abdallah Karaka, Pradyumna Bawankule, Weibin Li, Pierluigi Gargiani, Wouter Jolie, Arkady V. Krasheninnikov, Amilcar Bedoya-Pinto, Thomas Michely, Jeison Fischer
**Published:** 2026-04-08
**Summary:** Researchers demonstrate the first single-layer A-type antiferromagnet, Cr2S3-2D, and detect the switching of its Néel vector using spin-polarized STM, enabled by a substrate-induced magnetic moment imbalance.

**What's new**
- First experimental realization of a single-layer A-type antiferromagnet (Cr2S3-2D).
- Direct atomic-scale detection of Néel vector switching via spin-polarized STM hysteresis loops.
- Substrate-induced magnetic moment imbalance creates a net magnetization, enabling 180° Néel vector rotation.
- Material shows air stability, retaining magnetic properties after days of exposure.
- Combined XMCD and first-principles calculations confirm a nearly compensated AFM ground state with TN ~160 K.
