### [Mixture-of-Depths Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15619v1)
**Authors:** Lianghui Zhu, Yuxin Fang, Bencheng Liao, Shijie Wang, Tianheng Cheng, Zilong Huang, Chen Chen, Lai Wei, Yutao Zeng, Ya Wang, Yi Lin, Yu Li, Xinggang Wang
**Published:** 2026-03-16
**Summary:** A new attention mechanism that allows heads to attend to both current-layer and previous-layer key-value pairs, mitigating signal degradation in deep LLMs with minimal computational overhead.

**What's new**
- Heads attend to both current-layer sequence KV pairs and depth KV pairs from preceding layers.
- Includes a hardware-efficient algorithm achieving 97.3% of FlashAttention-2's efficiency at 64K sequence length.
- Improves average perplexity by 0.2 across 10 benchmarks and downstream task performance by 2.11% for a 1.5B model.
- Adds only a 3.7% FLOPs overhead and works better with post-norm than pre-norm architectures.

### [HorizonMath: Measuring AI Progress Toward Mathematical Discovery with Automatic Verification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15617v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-16
**Summary:** HorizonMath is a benchmark of unsolved math problems for evaluating AI's ability to make novel discoveries, with automated verification to prevent data contamination.

**What's new**
- Introduces over 100 unsolved problems across 8 math domains where verification is easy but discovery requires insight.
- Provides an open-source framework for automated solution verification, avoiding costly manual or formal proof review.
- Shows top models score near 0%, ensuring immunity to data contamination from training on known solutions.

### [Mechanistic Origin of Moral Indifference in Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15615v1)
**Authors:** Lingyu Li, Yan Teng, Yingchun Wang
**Published:** 2026-03-16
**Summary:** Identifies moral indifference in LLMs as the compression of distinct moral concepts into uniform representations and proposes a method to align latent moral features, improving reasoning.

**What's new**
- Posits that LLMs have inherent moral indifference due to compressing distinct moral concepts into uniform probability distributions.
- Analyzes 23 models, finding they fail to distinguish opposed moral categories or fine-grained typicality gradients.
- Constructs 251k moral vectors using Prototype Theory and the Social-Chemistry-101 dataset for verification.
- Uses Sparse Autoencoders on Qwen3-8B to isolate and topologically reconstruct mono-semantic moral features.
- Achieves a 75% pairwise win-rate on the adversarial Flames benchmark after representational alignment.
