# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-03-14

## What's New Today

**AI Agents Advance in Reasoning and Safety, Materials Science Reveals Hidden Phases**

Today's research spans from improving the strategic reasoning and safety of AI agents to uncovering hidden quantum phases in materials. New benchmarks and frameworks rigorously test multimodal agents on complex tasks, while materials simulations reveal novel polar states and altermagnetic phenomena. Foundational AI work also advances representation learning and model verification.

**Highlights**
- New benchmarks evaluate multimodal agents on strategic reasoning, safety, and scientific document comprehension.
- Materials studies discover hidden polar phases in SrTiO3 and persistent altermagnetism in novel compounds.
- Frameworks improve AI agent reasoning via chain-of-thought, temporal planning, and continual learning.
- Training-free methods emerge for efficient inference, model pruning, and guided visual generation.
- Proof-carrying materials framework offers formal safety certificates for machine-learned interatomic potentials.
- Cross-context review significantly boosts LLM self-correction accuracy in error detection tasks.

### [Emergent Anomalous Hall Effect from Surface States in the Altermagnet MnTe Thin Films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12259v1)
**Authors:** Yufei Zhao, Saswata Mandal, Chao-Xing Liu, Binghai Yan
**Published:** 2026-03-12
**Summary:** Surface states, not bulk, dominate the anomalous Hall effect in altermagnetic MnTe thin films, with sign and magnitude controllable by interface chemistry.

**What's new**
- Identifies surface states as the primary source of anomalous Hall effect in MnTe, resolving conflicting experimental reports.
- Shows surface magnetization follows the surface spin sublattice but the Hall effect is determined by bulk Neel order.
- Reveals a Te capping layer can reverse the sign of the surface anomalous Hall effect compared to an InP substrate.
- Provides a framework for engineering the Hall response in altermagnets via interface and termination design.
