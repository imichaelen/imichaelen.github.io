# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-04-17

## What's New Today

**AI Advances in Reasoning, Agents, and Materials; Physics Insights from Topology and Transport**

Today's research spans AI advancements in long-horizon reasoning, agentic systems, and robust reward modeling, alongside materials and physics discoveries in topological states and quantum transport. Self-evolving frameworks and multi-agent automation push the boundaries of spatial intelligence and complex workflow execution. In condensed matter, novel heterostructures and altermagnets reveal exotic quantum geometric effects.

- SpatialEvo introduces a self-evolving framework for 3D spatial reasoning using deterministic geometric environments to avoid error reinforcement.

- Twist-engineering induces a robust quantum spin Hall phase in a β-/flat bismuthene bilayer heterostructure.
- Altermagnet RuO2 thin films exhibit giant room-temperature third-order electrical transport from quantum geometry.
- MolCryst-MLIPs database releases fine-tuned machine-learned interatomic potentials for nine molecular crystal systems.

### [SpatialEvo: Self-Evolving Spatial Intelligence via Deterministic Geometric Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14144v1)
**Authors:** Dinging Li, Yingxiu Zhao, Xinrui Cheng, Kangheng Lin, Hongbo Peng, Hongxing Li, Zixuan Wang, Yuhong Dai, Haodong Li, Jia Wang, Yukang Shi, Liang Zhao, Jianjian Sun, Zheng Ge, Xiangyu Zhang, Weiming Lu, Jun Xiao, Yueting Zhuang, Yongliang Shen
**Published:** 2026-04-15
**Summary:** SpatialEvo is a self-evolving framework for 3D spatial reasoning that uses deterministic geometric environments to generate noise-free training data, eliminating reliance on model consensus.

**What's new**
- Introduces Deterministic Geometric Environment (DGE) with explicit validation rules for 16 spatial tasks, providing zero-noise ground truth from geometry.
- Replaces model consensus with objective physical feedback, preventing reinforcement of the model's own errors during self-training.
- Uses a single policy co-evolving in questioner and solver roles, generating valid questions and deriving precise answers under DGE constraints.
- Implements a task-adaptive scheduler that automatically focuses training on the model's weakest categories without manual curriculum design.

### [LongCoT: Benchmarking Long-Horizon Chain-of-Thought Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14140v1)

**Published:** 2026-04-15
**Summary:** LongCoT is a benchmark of 2,500 expert problems requiring long, multi-step reasoning to directly measure and expose limitations in current language models' long-horizon chain-of-thought capabilities.

**What's new**
- Introduces a scalable benchmark of 2,500 expert problems across domains like chemistry, math, and chess.
- Problems require navigating graphs of interdependent steps spanning tens to hundreds of thousands of reasoning tokens.
- Designed so each local step is tractable, isolating failures to long-horizon reasoning limitations.
- At release, best models like GPT-5.2 and Gemini 3 Pro show low accuracy (<10%), revealing a major capability gap.
