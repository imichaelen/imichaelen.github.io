# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-04-18

## What's New Today

**AI Agents Advance in Reasoning, Safety, and Efficiency; Materials Research Explores Novel Phases**

Today's research highlights significant progress in AI agent frameworks, with new systems for multimodal webpage generation, medical image interpretation, and autonomous RTL optimization. Concurrently, studies on systematic generalization and LLM-as-judge reliability reveal critical limitations in current models. In materials science, novel altermagnetic domains and ferroelectric phases are characterized, offering pathways for next-generation spintronics and memory.

**Highlights**
- MM-WebAgent introduces a hierarchical agentic framework for generating coherent, multimodal webpages.
- RadAgent demonstrates stepwise, interpretable chest CT report generation, improving over 3D VLM baselines.
- Studies show LLMs fail systematic length scaling in shortest-path problems and exhibit inconsistency as automated judges.
- Altermagnetic domains in MnTe are visualized via magneto-optical imaging, showing controllability without net magnetization.
- A vertical complementary FeRAM architecture achieves ultra-high polarization via all-atomic-layer deposition.
- Optically induced phase transitions in 2D ferroelectrics enable reversible switching between multiple ferroic states.

### [Generalization in LLM Problem Solving: The Case of the Shortest Path](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15306v1)
**Authors:** Yao Tong, Jiayuan Ye, Anastasia Borovykh, Reza Shokri
**Published:** 2026-04-16
**Summary:** A controlled study on LLMs solving shortest-path problems reveals strong spatial generalization but consistent failure in scaling to longer paths due to recursive instability.

**What's new**
- Introduces a synthetic shortest-path environment to isolate factors like data, training, and inference affecting generalization.
- Finds LLMs generalize well to unseen maps (spatial transfer) but fail on longer paths (length scaling) from recursive errors.
- Shows data coverage limits capabilities; RL improves stability but doesn't expand limits; inference scaling can't fix length failures.

### [Diagnosing LLM Judge Reliability: Conformal Prediction Sets and Transitivity Violations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15302v1)
**Authors:** Manan Gupta, Dhruv Kumar
**Published:** 2026-04-16
**Summary:** A diagnostic toolkit reveals per-instance unreliability in LLM-as-judge frameworks via transitivity violations and conformal prediction sets, showing criteria like relevance are more reliably judged than fluency.

**What's new**
- Transitivity analysis uncovers widespread per-input inconsistency in LLM judges, with 33-67% of documents showing directed 3-cycles.
- Uses split conformal prediction sets for Likert scores to provide per-instance reliability indicators with theoretical coverage guarantees.
- Shows prediction set width correlates with document difficulty, not judge noise, with cross-judge agreement (r=0.32-0.38).
- Finds criterion matters more than judge: relevance is most reliable, coherence moderate, fluency and consistency unreliable.
