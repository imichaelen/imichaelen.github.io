# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-03-11

## What's New Today

**AI Agents Advance in Reasoning and Safety, Materials Science Explores Novel Quantum States**

Today's research highlights significant progress in AI agent training and evaluation, with new frameworks for autonomous reasoning, safety alignment, and financial tool use. In parallel, materials physics reveals novel quantum behaviors in vanadium mosaic lattices and advances in nonadiabatic dynamics simulation. Cross-cutting themes include efficient model scaling and robust uncertainty quantification.

**Highlights**
- Agentic Critical Training (ACT) introduces a reinforcement learning paradigm to train LLM agents to identify better actions among alternatives.
- Impermanent benchmark evaluates forecasting models under open-world temporal change via sequential scoring on live data streams.
- Cs3V9Te13 compound discovered with a unique 2D vanadium mosaic lattice, exhibiting heavy-fermion behavior and a tunable density wave.
- FinToolBench introduced as the first real-world, runnable benchmark for evaluating LLM agents on financial tool execution.
- Scale Space Diffusion formalizes connection between diffusion models and scale-space theory, proposing more efficient processing.
- Nonadiabatic Transition Path Sampling (NATPS) enables path ensemble sampling for excited-state processes using time-reversible dynamics.

### [Agentic Critical Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08706v1)
**Authors:** Weize Liu, Minghui Liu, Sy-Tuyen Ho, Souradip Chakraborty, Xiyao Wang, Furong Huang
**Published:** 2026-03-09
**Summary:** Agentic Critical Training (ACT) is a reinforcement learning method that trains LLM agents to judge action quality autonomously, improving performance over imitation and reinforcement learning.

**What's new**
- Introduces ACT, a reinforcement learning paradigm that trains agents to identify better actions among alternatives.
- Rewards correct judgments to drive autonomous reasoning about action quality, not imitation of reflection text.
- Achieves average improvements of 5.07 points over imitation learning and 4.62 points over reinforcement learning.
- Enables strong out-of-distribution generalization on agentic benchmarks and boosts general reasoning performance.

**Published:** 2026-03-09
**Summary:** Unsupervised RL for LLMs using intrinsic rewards inevitably collapses by sharpening the model's initial biases, but external rewards may offer a scalable alternative.

**What's new**
- Establishes a theoretical framework showing all intrinsic reward methods converge to sharpen the model's initial distribution, leading to collapse.

### [Reasoning as Compression: Unifying Budget Forcing via the Conditional Information Bottleneck](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08462v1)
**Authors:** Fabio Valerio Massoli, Andrey Kuzmin, Arash Behboodi
**Published:** 2026-03-09
**Summary:** This paper reframes efficient reasoning in LLMs as a compression problem using the Conditional Information Bottleneck, unifying budget forcing methods to prune filler while preserving essential logic.

**What's new**
- Introduces Conditional Information Bottleneck to model reasoning as a computational bridge, addressing attention's violation of Markov property.
- Derives a general RL objective subsuming heuristic length penalties as special cases under a prior over reasoning traces.

- Empirically shows the method prunes cognitive bloat, improving accuracy at moderate compression with minimal drop at aggressive levels.
