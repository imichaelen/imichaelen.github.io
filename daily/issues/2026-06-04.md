### [Neuron Populations Exhibit Divergent Selectivity with Scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03990v1)
**Authors:** Amil Dravid, Yasaman Bahri, Alexei A. Efros, Yossi Gandelsman
**Published:** 2026-06-02
**Summary:** Neuron populations in neural networks follow a sublinear power law with scale, becoming more selective and monosemantic.

**What's new**
- Rosetta Neurons grow in absolute number but shrink as a fraction of total neurons with model size.
- Neuron Polarization Effect: Rosetta Neurons become more selective and monosemantic, separating from less selective non-Rosetta neurons.
- An analytical model balancing feature utility and neuron capacity explains the sublinear scaling and polarization.
- Rosetta Neurons become more domain-specialized with scale, demonstrated via a data-filtering case study.
