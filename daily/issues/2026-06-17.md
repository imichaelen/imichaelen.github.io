## Featured Papers

### [The Value Axis: Language Models Encode Whether They're on the Right Track](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17056v1)
**Authors:** Nick Jiang, Isaac Kauvar, Jack Lindsey
**Published:** 2026-06-15
**Summary:** Language models encode a 'value axis' tracking trajectory success likelihood, influencing confidence and behavior.

**What's new**
- Constructed a value axis in Qwen3-8B using synthetic in-context RL data.
- Axis activations distinguish high vs. low confidence, backtracking, and correct vs. corrupted code.
- Steering along axis causally modulates self-correction, verbosity, and exploration.
- DPO increases internal value for rewarded behaviors, boosting confidence.
- Applied to real-world settings: low value for sensitive queries post-training, SFT boosts in-domain confidence.

### [Context-Aware RL for Agentic and Multimodal LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17053v1)
**Authors:** Peiyang Xu, Bangzheng Li, Sijia Liu, Karthik R. Narasimhan, Pramod Viswanath, Prateek Mittal, Xingyu Fu
**Published:** 2026-06-15
**Summary:** ContextRL uses context-aware RL to improve LLM reasoning by rewarding selection of supporting context, achieving gains on long-horizon and multimodal tasks.

**What's new**
- Introduces an indirect auxiliary objective that rewards context selection over final answer supervision.
- Constructs 1K contrastive pairs for coding agents and 7K for multimodal reasoning via generative editing.
- Achieves +2.2% on long-horizon benchmarks and +1.8% on 12 VQA benchmarks over standard GRPO.
- Shows gains come from the context-selection objective, not just additional contrastive data.

### [Exact Posterior Score Estimation for Solving Linear Inverse Problems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17048v1)
**Authors:** Abbas Mammadov, Ozgur Kara, Kaan Oktay, Iskander Azangulov, Adil Kaan Akan, Hyungjin Chung, James Matthew Rehg, Yee Whye Teh
**Published:** 2026-06-15
**Summary:** Exact Posterior Score (EPS) enables solving linear inverse problems by deriving the exact posterior score for diffusion/flow models.

**What's new**
- Derives closed-form exact posterior score for linear Gaussian inverse problems under general Gaussian interpolants.
- Introduces EPS training objective that preserves standard denoising structure, usable from scratch or fine-tuned.

### [Benchmarking LLM Agents on Meta-Analysis Articles from Nature Portfolio](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17041v1)
**Authors:** Anzhe Xie, Weihang Su, Yujia Zhou, Yiqun Liu, Qingyao Ai
**Published:** 2026-06-15
**Summary:** MetaSyn benchmark reveals LLM agents struggle to screen PI/ECO-eligible studies in meta-analysis, despite strong retrieval recall.

**What's new**
- Introduces MetaSyn dataset with 442 expert-curated meta-analyses from Nature Portfolio.
- Each entry includes PI/ECO criteria, 140k PubMed corpus, verified positives, and hard negatives.

### [The Importance of Phase in Neural Representations: An Internal Oppenheim-Lim Test of Image Classifiers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17037v1)
**Authors:** Alper Yıldırım
**Published:** 2026-06-15
**Summary:** Image classifiers encode object identity in Fourier phase/sign of hidden representations, not magnitude, mirroring a classic perceptual finding.

**What's new**
- Causal test transplants phase/sign between images at hidden layers to track identity.
- ViT, GFNet, PRISM2D follow phase donor; ResNet-50 hides sign code before ReLU.
- Deleting image-specific magnitude barely affects classifier predictions.
- ResNet-50's late blocks show latent sign code, readout uses channel-wise spatial average.
- Explains texture-shape gap between CNNs and attention models via phase coding.
