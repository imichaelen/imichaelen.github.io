## What's New Today

**AI Advances in Reasoning, Safety, and Efficiency; Materials Insights from Simulation and ML**

Today's research spans core AI advancements in reasoning, safety, and system efficiency alongside materials science discoveries powered by simulation and machine learning. New frameworks for reinforcement learning, uncertainty quantification, and model alignment aim to make AI systems more robust and controllable. In parallel, atomistic simulations and ML interatomic potentials provide new insights into alloy deformation, catalysis, and quantum chemistry workflows.

**Highlights**
- Reinforced Attention Learning (RAL) optimizes MLLM attention distributions, not outputs, for improved multimodal grounding.
- Divergence Proximal Optimization proposed as a fix for PPO's structural issues with LLM vocabularies.
- Bond Smoothness Characterization Test (BSCT) offers efficient evaluation of ML interatomic potential physicality.
- Study finds LLM Chain-of-Thought can internally propagate unsafe narratives even when final output refuses.

### [Atomistic and data-driven insights into the local slip resistances in random refractory multi-principal element alloys](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04827v1)

**Summary:** Cross-sectional helium irradiation shows Cr/CrAlSiN multilayer coatings on zirconium alloys suppress bubble accumulation and swelling via nanochannel interfaces and a protective nitride interlayer.

**What's new**
- Used cross-sectional He irradiation to directly probe interfacial bubble evolution in coatings, overcoming limitations of surface irradiation.

### [Triple Junctions as Dislocation-Like Defects: The Role of Grain Boundary Crystallography Revealed by Experiment and Atomistic Simulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04530v1)

**Published:** 2026-02-04
**Summary:** Atomic-scale study reveals triple junctions in aluminum act like dislocations, with a measurable Burgers vector and energy, and the observed junction is not the lowest-energy configuration.

**What's new**
- Developed a technique to calculate the Burgers vector of a triple junction, linking its dislocation character to the grain boundaries' microscopic degrees of freedom.
- Showed the triple junction line energy follows the same laws as a bulk dislocation, calculated using an atomistic model.
- Found a range of possible triple junctions for the observed boundaries, varying in Burgers vector magnitude.
- The experimentally observed junction was not the one with the smallest Burgers vector and energy, suggesting slow transformation kinetics.
