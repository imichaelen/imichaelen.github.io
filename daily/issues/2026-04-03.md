### [HippoCamp: Benchmarking Contextual Agents on Personal Computers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01221v1)
**Authors:** Zhe Yang, Shulin Tian, Kairui Hu, Shuai Liu, Hoang-Nhat Nguyen, Yichi Zhang, Zujin Guo, Mengying Yu, Zinan Zhang, Jingkang Yang, Chen Change Loy, Ziwei Liu
**Published:** 2026-04-01
**Summary:** HippoCamp is a benchmark for evaluating AI agents on multimodal file management tasks in realistic personal computing environments.

**What's new**
- Introduces a benchmark with 581 QA pairs based on 42.4 GB of real-world personal files across diverse modalities.
- Focuses on user-centric tasks like personal profile modeling and context-aware search within dense file systems.
- Provides 46.1K densely annotated structured trajectories for step-wise failure diagnosis of agent reasoning.
- Reveals a major performance gap, with top models achieving only 48.3% accuracy on user profiling tasks.
- Identifies multimodal perception and evidence grounding as primary bottlenecks for current AI agents.

### [$\texttt{YC-Bench}$: Benchmarking AI Agents for Long-Term Planning and Consistent Execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01212v1)
**Authors:** Muyu He, Adit Jain, Anand Kumar, Vincent Tu, Soumyadeep Bakshi, Sachin Patro, Nazneen Rajani
**Published:** 2026-04-01
**Summary:** YC-Bench is a benchmark for evaluating AI agents' long-term planning and execution by simulating a startup over a year, testing strategic coherence under uncertainty and compounding consequences.

**What's new**
- Introduces a benchmark simulating a startup over a one-year horizon with hundreds of turns to test long-term planning under uncertainty.
- Finds only three models consistently surpass starting capital, with Claude Opus 4.6 leading but GLM-5 close at much lower cost.
- Identifies scratchpad usage as the strongest success predictor and adversarial client detection as the main failure mode.
- Reveals distinct failure modes like over-parallelization, highlighting gaps in long-horizon agent performance.
- Provides an open-source, reproducible, and configurable benchmark for evaluating AI agents on complex, multi-step tasks.
