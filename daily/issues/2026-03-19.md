# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-03-19

## What's New Today

**AI Reasoning, Vision-Language Models, and Materials Science Advance in Daily Research**

Research today spans from uncovering the inner workings of AI reasoning in video models to mitigating hallucinations in vision-language systems. New datasets and benchmarks push the boundaries of multimodal evaluation and low-resource language support. In materials science, novel methods address phonon dynamics and defect migration with machine-learned potentials.

**Highlights**
- Video diffusion models reason via Chain-of-Steps in denoising, not sequentially across frames.

- Machine-learned interatomic potentials reveal ligand effects on phonons in perovskite nanocrystals.
- Methods proposed to reduce positional bias in Vision Transformers for material science imagery.
- Conformal factuality for RAG-based LLMs is systematically analyzed for reliability and usefulness.

### [Demystifing Video Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16870v1)
**Authors:** Ruisi Wang, Zhongang Cai, Fanyi Pu, Junxiang Xu, Wanqi Yin, Maijunxian Wang, Ran Ji, Chenyang Gu, Bo Li, Ziqi Huang, Hokin Deng, Dahua Lin, Ziwei Liu, Lei Yang
**Published:** 2026-03-17
**Summary:** Challenges the Chain-of-Frames view, showing reasoning in video diffusion models emerges along denoising steps via a Chain-of-Steps mechanism with emergent behaviors like working memory.

**What's new**
- Proposes Chain-of-Steps: reasoning unfolds across diffusion denoising steps, not sequentially across video frames.
- Identifies key emergent reasoning behaviors: working memory, self-correction, and perception-before-action.
- Reveals functional specialization within Diffusion Transformer layers during a single denoising step.
- Demonstrates a training-free improvement strategy by ensembling latent trajectories from different random seeds.
