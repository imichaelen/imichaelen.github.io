# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-04-01

## What's New Today

**AI Advances in Quantization, Reasoning, and Agents; Materials Insights on Floquet Gaps and Catalysts**

Today's research spans efficient AI model design and robust reasoning systems alongside novel materials discoveries. New adaptive data types improve 4-bit LLM quantization, while frameworks enhance the reliability of agentic and multimodal systems. In materials science, direct observation of Floquet-induced gaps in graphene and graphite is reported, and a pentagonal PdTe2 monolayer is identified as a tunable photocatalyst.

- IF4 data type adaptively selects FP4 or INT4 per block for improved 4-bit LLM quantization.

- Pentagonal PdTe2 monolayer shows strain-tunable band edges for spontaneous water splitting.
- Stepwise credit assignment (Stepwise-Flow-GRPO) boosts RL efficiency for diffusion models.
- Temporal credit assignment in RNNs shown to be possible without Jacobian propagation.

### [Adaptive Block-Scaled Data Types](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28765v1)
**Authors:** Jack Cook, Hyemin S. Lee, Kathryn Le, Junxian Guo, Giovanni Traverso, Anantha P. Chandrakasan, Song Han
**Published:** 2026-03-30
**Summary:** Proposes IF4, a new 4-bit quantization format that adaptively chooses between FP4 and INT4 per block to reduce error, outperforming NVFP4 for LLMs.

**What's new**
- Introduces IF4, an adaptive 4-bit format that selects FP4 or INT4 per block using NVFP4's unused sign bit.
- Extends the adaptive block-scaled concept to other bit-widths like IF3 and IF6.
- Shows IF4 outperforms existing 4-bit formats in quantized training loss and post-training accuracy for LLMs.
- Designs and evaluates a hardware-efficient IF4 Multiply-Accumulate (MAC) unit for accelerators.

**What's new**

### [Stop Probing, Start Coding: Why Linear Probes and Sparse Autoencoders Fail at Compositional Generalisation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28744v1)
**Authors:** Vitória Barin Pacela, Shruti Joshi, Isabela Camacho, Simon Lacoste-Julien, David Klindt
**Published:** 2026-03-30
**Summary:** Sparse autoencoders fail at compositional generalization due to poor dictionary learning, not amortization, as their learned dictionaries misalign with true concepts under superposition.

**What's new**
- Identifies dictionary learning, not amortized inference, as the core reason sparse autoencoders fail under out-of-distribution compositional shifts.
- Shows the amortization gap persists regardless of training size, latent dimensions, or sparsity, and replacing the encoder with per-sample inference doesn't fix it.
- Demonstrates the problem is solvable with a good dictionary, reframing the challenge as scalable dictionary learning for sparse inference under superposition.

### [Pentagonal PdTe2 Monolayer for Sustainable Solar-driven Hydrogen Production](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28726v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-30
**Summary:** A pentagonal PdTe2 monolayer is a tunable 2D photocatalyst where +3% tensile strain enables efficient solar-driven water splitting with 20.4% STH efficiency.

**What's new**
- Identifies pentagonal PdTe2 monolayer as a tunable photocatalyst with a 1.75 eV bandgap and high hole mobility.

### [Observation of Floquet-induced gap in graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28725v1)
**Authors:** Fei Wang, Xuanxi Cai, Xiao Tang, Jinxi Lu, Wanying Chen, Tianshuang Sheng, Runfa Feng, Haoyuan Zhong, Hongyun Zhang, Pu Yu, Shuyun Zhou
**Published:** 2026-03-30
**Summary:** First direct observation of a light-induced hybridization gap in graphene's electronic structure using time-resolved photoemission, confirming a key prediction of Floquet engineering.

**What's new**
- Direct spectroscopic observation of a Floquet-induced gap at band crossings in graphene under strong resonant light.
- Reveals momentum-anisotropic gap with two Dirac nodes protected by spatiotemporal symmetry.
- Demonstrates tunability of the gap and Dirac node positions via light polarization.
- Provides long-sought experimental validation of the foundational Floquet band engineering concept in graphene.
