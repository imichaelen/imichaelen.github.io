### [Key Role of Charge Disproportionation in Monoclinic Semiconducting Fe$_2$PO$_5$, a Room-Temperature d-Wave Altermagnet Candidate](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06114v1)

**Published:** 2026-04-07
**Summary:** Fe2PO5 is a room-temperature d-wave altermagnetic semiconductor, stabilized by charge disproportionation and monoclinic distortion driven by electronic correlations.

**What's new**
- Identifies charge disproportionation as the key mechanism driving the monoclinic distortion and semiconducting gap in Fe2PO5.

### [Shot-Based Quantum Encoding: A Data-Loading Paradigm for Quantum Neural Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06135v1)
**Authors:** Basil Kyriacou, Viktoria Patapovich, Maniraman Periyasamy, Alexey Melnikov
**Published:** 2026-04-07
**Summary:** Shot-Based Quantum Encoding (SBQE) is a data-loading method for quantum neural networks that uses shot counts as a learnable resource to create mixed-state representations, avoiding encoding gates and improving accuracy.

**What's new**
- Encodes data by distributing hardware shots according to a classical distribution over multiple initial quantum states, using shot counts as a learnable degree of freedom.
- Produces mixed-state representations with expectation values linear in classical probabilities, enabling composition with non-linear activation functions.
- Structurally equivalent to a multilayer perceptron with weights realized by quantum circuits, offering a hardware-compatible implementation protocol.
- Achieves improved test accuracy on benchmarks like Fashion MNIST and Semeion digits compared to amplitude encoding and classical networks, without data-encoding gates.
