### [MedObvious: Exposing the Medical Moravec's Paradox in VLMs via Clinical Triage](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23501v1)
**Authors:** Ufaq Khan, Umair Nawaz, L D M S S Teja, Numaan Saeed, Muhammad Bilal, Yutong Xie, Mohammad Yaqub, Muhammad Haris Khan
**Published:** 2026-03-24
**Summary:** MedObvious is a benchmark exposing that medical VLMs often fail basic sanity checks on image inputs, producing plausible text even when visuals are invalid, a critical safety gap.

**What's new**
- Introduces MedObvious, a 1,880-task benchmark isolating input validation via multi-panel image set consistency checks.
- Spans five tiers from basic mismatches to clinical anatomy/viewpoint verification and triage-style cues.
- Evaluates 17 VLMs, finding unreliable sanity checking, hallucination on normal inputs, and performance drops with more images.
- Shows accuracy varies widely between multiple-choice and open-ended formats, highlighting evaluation sensitivity.
- Argues pre-diagnostic verification is a distinct, unsolved safety-critical capability for medical VLM deployment.
