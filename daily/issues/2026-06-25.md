### [New Bounds for the Last Iterate of the Stochastic subGradient Method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24879v1)
**Authors:** Guglielmo Beretta, Tommaso Cesari, Roberto Colomboni, Andrea Paudice
**Published:** 2026-06-23
**Summary:** Proves last iterate of stochastic subgradient method achieves O(1/√n) error under i.i.d. noise, but O(log n/√n) without it, resolving an open problem.

**What's new**
- Shows last iterate optimization error of O(1/√n) for i.i.d. subgradient noise with bounded variance.
- Proves without i.i.d. assumption, error grows to O(log n/√n), showing suboptimality even in 1D.
- Resolves open problem from Koren and Segal (COLT 2020) on last iterate optimality.

### [Mechanism of Band Gap Formation in Beam Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24860v1)
**Authors:** Kwangmin Lee, Charles Emmett Maher, Katherine A. Newhall, Ryan C. Hurley
**Published:** 2026-06-23
**Summary:** Band gaps in beam networks arise from geometry-induced axial-bending coupling, not Bragg scattering or local resonance.

**What's new**
- Band gap onset is due to axial-bending coupling at lattice nodes, scaling with the axial cutoff frequency of a 1D periodic beam.
- Band gap termination is governed by high-frequency rotational branches linked to beam geometry.
- The mechanism applies to both periodic and disordered beam networks, manifesting via beam orientations or short-beam statistics.
