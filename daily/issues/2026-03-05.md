### [CFG-Ctrl: Control-Based Classifier-Free Diffusion Guidance](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03281v1)
**Authors:** Hanyang Wang, Yiyang Liu, Jiawei Chi, Fangfu Liu, Ran Xue, Yueqi Duan
**Published:** 2026-03-03
**Summary:** CFG-Ctrl reinterprets classifier-free guidance as a control problem, introducing a sliding mode control variant (SMC-CFG) for more stable and semantically aligned image generation.

**What's new**
- Frames CFG as a control system, identifying vanilla CFG as a proportional controller with fixed gain.
- Proposes SMC-CFG using a nonlinear sliding mode surface for rapid convergence and reduced overshooting.
- Provides Lyapunov stability analysis to guarantee finite-time convergence of the generative flow.
- Demonstrates improved semantic alignment and robustness across guidance scales in models like Stable Diffusion 3.5.

### [Anisotropic magnetoelastic coupling in the honeycomb magnet Na$_3$Co$_2$SbO$_6$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03214v1)
**Authors:** Prashanta K. Mukharjee, Sebastian Erdmann, Lichen Wang, Julian Kaiser, Anton Jesche, Pascal Puphal, Masahiko Isobe, Matthias Hepting, Bernhard Keimer, Philipp Gegenwart, Alexander A. Tsirlin
**Published:** 2026-03-03
**Summary:** Magnetization and dilatometry measurements reveal anisotropic magnetoelastic coupling and field-induced transitions in honeycomb cobaltate Na3Co2SbO6, with no evidence of a quantum spin liquid state.

**What's new**
- Strongly anisotropic c-axis lattice response dominated by Co-O-Co bond angle variations, per ab initio calculations.
- Step-like magnetization features at 0.4 K with high anisotropy; first-order transition indicated by hysteresis for B ∥ b.
- Suppressed divergence of magnetic Grüneisen parameter at Bc2 upon cooling, signaling absence of quantum criticality.
- No thermodynamic evidence for a field-induced quantum spin liquid state near or above the second transition field Bc2.

### [Beyond Task Completion: Revealing Corrupt Success in LLM Agents through Procedure-Aware Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03116v1)
**Authors:** Hongliu Cao, Ilias Driouich, Eoin Thomas
**Published:** 2026-03-03
**Summary:** Introduces Procedure-Aware Evaluation (PAE) to assess LLM agents beyond task completion, revealing widespread 'corrupt successes' where tasks succeed but procedures are violated.

**What's new**
- PAE framework evaluates agents on Utility, Efficiency, Interaction Quality, and Procedural Integrity with multi-dimensional gating.
- Exposes that 27-78% of benchmark successes are corrupt, hiding violations in interaction and integrity.
- Reveals non-redundant failure modes: utility masks reliability, speed doesn't imply precision.
- Shows distinct model failure patterns, like GPT-5's spread errors and Kimi-K2's policy violations.
- Identifies structural benchmark flaws like task gaps and contradictory rewards that allow accidental success.

**Summary:** A generalized Bayesian framework for causal inference that places priors directly on causal estimands and updates them via loss functions, enabling uncertainty quantification without full likelihood modeling.

**What's new**
