## Featured Papers

### [TriAttention: Efficient Long Reasoning with Trigonometric KV Compression](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04921v1)
**Authors:** Weian Mao, Xi Lin, Wei Huang, Yuxin Xie, Tianfu Fu, Bohan Zhuang, Song Han, Yukang Chen
**Published:** 2026-04-06
**Summary:** TriAttention compresses KV cache by using stable pre-RoPE query/key concentration to score keys via trigonometric distance preferences, enabling efficient long reasoning.

**What's new**
- Identifies Q/K concentration in pre-RoPE space, where vectors cluster around fixed centers stable across positions.
- Uses trigonometric series from these centers to score keys by position distance preference for importance estimation.
- Incorporates Q/K norms as an additional signal to improve key importance scoring accuracy.
- Achieves 2.5x higher throughput or 10.7x KV memory reduction while matching full attention accuracy on long tasks.
- Enables long-context model deployment on a single consumer GPU where full attention would cause out-of-memory.

### [Early Stopping for Large Reasoning Models via Confidence Dynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04930v1)
**Authors:** Parsa Hosseini, Sumit Nawathe, Mahdi Salmani, Meisam Razaviyayn, Soheil Feizi
**Published:** 2026-04-06
**Summary:** CoDE-Stop uses intermediate answer confidence dynamics to decide when to halt chain-of-thought reasoning, reducing compute without extra training.

**What's new**
- Proposes CoDE-Stop, an early stopping method based on confidence dynamics of intermediate answers during reasoning.
- Observes correct reasoning reaches high confidence early, while incorrect reasoning shows unreliable confidence patterns.
- Reduces total token usage by 25-50% compared to standard full-length reasoning across diverse benchmarks.
- Achieves better accuracy-compute tradeoff than prior early stopping methods with no additional training required.
- Provides analysis of confidence dynamics in both correct and incorrect reasoning trajectories.

### [Proton Quantum Effects in H$_3$S Electronic Structure: A Multicomponent DFT study via Nuclear-Electronic Orbital Method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04860v1)
**Authors:** Jianhang Xu, Aaron M. Schankler, Yosuke Kanai
**Published:** 2026-04-06
**Summary:** Nuclear quantum effects in H3S slightly modify electronic structure but significantly stiffen hydrogen phonons, explaining the Tc reduction upon deuteration.

**What's new**
- Uses NEO-DFT to treat hydrogen nuclei quantum mechanically alongside electrons.
- Shows nuclear quantum effects induce only subtle changes in electronic band structure near Fermi level.
- Reveals these electronic changes would increase Tc by only a few percent.
- Finds large stiffening of S-H bonds and changes in hydrogen phonons due to nuclear quantum effects.
- Concludes deuteration reduces Tc primarily via phonon changes, not electronic structure modifications.

### [Engineering 2D high-temperature ferromagnets with large in-plane anisotropy via alkali-metal decoration in a tetragonal CoSe monolayer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04739v1)
**Authors:** Yiran Peng, Yanfeng Ge, Yong Liu, Wenhui Wan
**Published:** 2026-04-06
**Summary:** Alkali-metal decoration of a tetragonal CoSe monolayer creates stable 2D ferromagnets with Curie temperatures above 300 K and large in-plane magnetic anisotropy.

**What's new**
- Alkali atoms (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs) on CoSe monolayer dramatically enhance ferromagnetism, achieving Tc > 300 K and MAE > 800 μeV/Co.
- The decoration amplifies Co magnetic moments and reinforces ferromagnetic couplings while suppressing direct antiferromagnetic exchange.
- LiCoSe is identified as a half-metal, offering potential for fully spin-polarized transport.
- Tensile strain can further increase both the magnetic anisotropy energy and the Curie temperature.
- NaCoSe emerges as the most promising candidate due to its highest MAE and excellent strain-tunable Tc.

### [Multiferroicity in the Presence of Exchange Bias: The Case of Spinel CoMn2O4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04880v1)
**Authors:** P. Kumar, P. Das, B. K. Kuanr, S. Patnaik
**Published:** 2026-04-06
**Summary:** Spinel CoMn2O4 shows multiferroic-like magnetodielectric coupling and exchange bias, but lacks intrinsic ferroelectric order.

**What's new**
- Identifies two magnetic transitions at ~186 K and a Yafet-Kittel ferrimagnetic transition at ~86 K.

- Reveals the absence of intrinsic ferroelectric polarization through detailed pyroelectric measurements.
