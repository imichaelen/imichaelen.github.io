# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-04-11

## What's New Today

**AI Agents Grapple with Reasoning, Tools, and Alignment; Physics Models Advance Simulation and Materials**

Today's research highlights core challenges in aligning and reasoning with large AI models, from meta-cognitive deficits in agentic systems to the fragility of reasoning traces in multimodal models. In parallel, physics-aligned simulation and novel materials characterization offer new pathways for scalable robotics and functional materials discovery. Benchmarks and evaluation frameworks are proliferating to rigorously test these emerging capabilities across domains.

**Highlights**
- Agentic multimodal models suffer from a 'meta-cognitive deficit', blindly invoking tools even when visual context suffices.
- A physics-aligned simulator, SIM1, is proposed as a zero-shot data scaler for robotic manipulation with deformable objects.
- Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts models exhibit 'Seeing but Not Thinking', where visual perception is accurate but subsequent reasoning fails.
- Hard-constrained PINNs offer a new formulation for interface problems, embedding physics directly into the solution representation.
- A new benchmark, CrashSight, evaluates vision-language models on infrastructure-centric traffic crash understanding.
- Machine unlearning methods may create an 'illusion' of forgetting, with discriminative features remaining in hidden layers.

### [Act Wisely: Cultivating Meta-Cognitive Tool Use in Agentic Multimodal Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08545v1)
**Authors:** Shilin Yan, Jintao Tong, Hongwei Xue, Xiaojun Tang, Yangyang Wang, Kunyu Shi, Guannan Zhang, Ruixuan Li, Yixiong Zou
**Published:** 2026-04-09
**Summary:** HDPO framework trains agentic multimodal models to use tools efficiently by decoupling accuracy and efficiency objectives, reducing unnecessary tool calls while improving task performance.

**What's new**
- Proposes HDPO, a framework that reframes tool efficiency as a conditional objective rather than a competing scalar reward.
- Decouples optimization into accuracy and efficiency channels, preventing the suppression of essential tool use.
- Introduces conditional advantage estimation to enforce execution economy only within accurate task trajectories.
- Results in Metis, a model that drastically reduces tool invocations while simultaneously improving reasoning accuracy.
- Creates a cognitive curriculum where agents master task resolution before refining self-reliance and tool arbitration.

### [SIM1: Physics-Aligned Simulator as Zero-Shot Data Scaler in Deformable Worlds](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08544v1)

**Published:** 2026-04-09
**Summary:** SIM1 is a physics-aligned simulator that uses limited real demonstrations to generate high-fidelity synthetic data for training robotic policies in deformable object manipulation.

**What's new**

- Achieves policy parity with real-data baselines at a 1:15 equivalence ratio using purely synthetic data.
- Enables 90% zero-shot success and 50% generalization gains in real-world deployment for deformable manipulation tasks.

### [Seeing but Not Thinking: Routing Distraction in Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08541v1)
**Authors:** Haolei Xu, Haiwen Hong, Hongxing Li, Rui Zhou, Yang Zhang, Longtao Huang, Hui Xue, Yongliang Shen, Weiming Lu, Yueting Zhuang
**Published:** 2026-04-09
**Summary:** Multimodal Mixture-of-Experts models accurately perceive images but fail at reasoning due to routing distraction, where visual inputs inadequately activate task-relevant experts.

**What's new**
- Identifies the Seeing but Not Thinking phenomenon: models perceive images correctly but fail at reasoning that succeeds with text.
- Reveals layer-wise separation of visual and domain experts, with image inputs causing routing divergence in middle layers.
- Proposes the Routing Distraction hypothesis: visual inputs inadequately activate task-relevant reasoning experts.
- Introduces a routing-guided intervention method to enhance domain expert activation, improving performance on reasoning tasks.

### [Demystifying OPD: Length Inflation and Stabilization Strategies for Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08527v1)

**Published:** 2026-04-09
**Summary:** Identifies and fixes a failure mode in on-policy distillation where training collapses due to length inflation and repetition in student rollouts.

**What's new**

### [Hard-constrained Physics-informed Neural Networks for Interface Problems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08453v1)
**Authors:** Seung Whan Chung, Stephen Castonguay, Sumanta Roy, Michael Penwarden, Yucheng Fu, Pratanu Roy
**Published:** 2026-04-09
**Summary:** Two hard-constrained PINN methods (windowing and buffer) that embed interface physics into the solution representation, improving accuracy and removing loss-weight tuning for interface problems.

**What's new**
- Introduces two ansatz-based hard-constrained PINN formulations that decouple interface enforcement from PDE residual minimization.
- Windowing approach uses compactly supported windowed subnetworks to satisfy interface conditions by design.
- Buffer approach augments subnetworks with auxiliary buffer functions that enforce constraints via a lightweight discrete correction.
- Hard constraints consistently improve interface fidelity and eliminate the need for loss-weight tuning in 1D problems.
- In 2D, the buffer method is more robust and geometrically flexible, while windowing becomes sensitive to overlap and corner effects.
