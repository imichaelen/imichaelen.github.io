## What's New Today

**AI Agents Advance in Driving, Reasoning, and Safety; Materials Research Reveals Novel Phenomena**

Today's research spans autonomous systems, AI reasoning, and materials science. Vision-language-action models are achieving personalized driving through instruction-following and preference alignment. In AI fundamentals, new methods improve reasoning consistency, knowledge base training, and efficient long-context generation. Materials studies demonstrate electrostatic control of photoluminescence, emergent superconductivity in bilayers, and single-atom magnets on stable surfaces.

- Vega and Drive My Way introduce personalized, instruction-following autonomous driving frameworks.

- Quantum confinement and proximity effects are predicted to induce superconductivity in non-superconducting bilayers.
- Single Dy atoms on NaCl(100) show magnetic bistability and long spin relaxation at thermally stable sites.

**Published:** 2026-03-26
**Summary:** A framework that treats the knowledge base in RAG systems as trainable, distilling fragmented facts from labeled examples into compact units and indexing them back to improve retrieval.

**What's new**
- Proposes WriteBack-RAG, a framework to train the knowledge base by distilling relevant facts from labeled examples.

### [PackForcing: Short Video Training Suffices for Long Video Sampling and Long Context Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25730v1)
**Authors:** Xiaofeng Mao, Shaohao Rui, Kaining Ying, Bo Zheng, Chuanhao Li, Mingmin Chi, Kaipeng Zhang
**Published:** 2026-03-26
**Summary:** PackForcing is a video diffusion framework that uses a three-partition KV-cache strategy to enable efficient long-video generation from short-video training.

**What's new**
- Introduces a three-partition KV-cache: Sink tokens for global semantics, compressed Mid tokens, and Recent tokens for local coherence.
- Achieves massive 32x token reduction for Mid tokens via a dual-branch network with 3D convolutions and VAE re-encoding.
- Employs dynamic top-k context selection and Temporal RoPE Adjustment to bound memory and maintain quality.
- Enables 2-minute video generation on a single GPU with a bounded 4 GB KV cache and 24x temporal extrapolation.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on VBench using only 5-second training clips for long-video synthesis.

### [Electrostatic Photoluminescence Tuning in All-Solid-State Perovskite Transistors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25718v1)
**Authors:** Vladimir Bruevich, Dmitry Maslennikov, Beier Hu, Artem A. Bakulin, Vitaly Podzorov
**Published:** 2026-03-26
**Summary:** An all-solid-state perovskite transistor uses a gate voltage to electrostatically control photoluminescence intensity by modulating non-radiative recombination.

**What's new**
- Gate voltage reversibly tunes perovskite photoluminescence intensity by 65-98%, not via electroluminescence.
- Electric field modulates interfacial charge density to affect radiative vs. non-radiative recombination channels.
- Nearly complete elimination of non-radiative losses is achievable at favorable gating conditions.
- High external photoluminescence quantum efficiency in large-area, thin-film devices due to epitaxial films.

### [Single Atom Magnets on Thermally Stable Adsorption Sites: Dy on NaCl(100)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25608v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-26
**Summary:** Single Dy atoms on NaCl(100) show magnetic bistability and long spin relaxation times, making NaCl a promising platform for single-atom magnets.

**What's new**
- First single-atom magnet on a thermally stable adsorption site, stable up to 300 K.
- Dy atoms substituting Na sites show 4f^9 occupancy, out-of-plane magnetization, and ~10 s spin relaxation at 2.5 K.

- Introduces NaCl as an effective insulating platform for single-atom magnets with slow magnetic relaxation.
