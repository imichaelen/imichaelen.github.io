### [Recursive Multi-Agent Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25917v1)
**Authors:** Xiyuan Yang, Jiaru Zou, Rui Pan, Ruizhong Qiu, Pan Lu, Shizhe Diao, Jindong Jiang, Hanghang Tong, Tong Zhang, Markus J. Buehler, Jingrui He, James Zou
**Published:** 2026-04-28
**Summary:** RecursiveMAS scales multi-agent collaboration through recursive computation, improving accuracy, speed, and token efficiency.

**What's new**
- Introduces RecursiveMAS, a framework treating multi-agent systems as unified latent-space recursive computation.
- Uses RecursiveLink module for cross-agent latent state transfer and in-distribution thought generation.
- Develops inner-outer loop learning for whole-system co-optimization via shared gradient-based credit assignment.
- Achieves 8.3% average accuracy improvement, 1.2x-2.4x speedup, and 34.6%-75.6% token reduction across 9 benchmarks.

### [Carbon-Taxed Transformers: A Green Compression Pipeline for Overgrown Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25903v1)
**Authors:** Ajmain Inqiad Alam, Palash Roy, Chanchal K. Roy, Banani Roy, Kevin A. Schneider
**Published:** 2026-04-28
**Summary:** Introduces Carbon-Taxed Transformers, a compression pipeline that penalizes inefficiency to reduce LLM size, speed, and emissions while preserving accuracy.

**What's new**
- Applies economic carbon taxation to penalize architectural inefficiencies in LLMs.
- Achieves up to 49x memory reduction and 81% CO2 emission cuts.
- Retains ~98% accuracy on code clone detection and ~89% on summarization.
- Validated across encoder-only, encoder-decoder, and decoder-only architectures.
- Ablation studies confirm pipeline ordering and component contributions are essential.

### [Conditional misalignment: common interventions can hide emergent misalignment behind contextual triggers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25891v1)
**Authors:** Jan Dubiński, Jan Betley, Anna Sztyber-Betley, Daniel Tan, Owain Evans
**Published:** 2026-04-28
**Summary:** Common interventions to reduce emergent misalignment in LLMs can hide it behind contextual triggers, leading to conditional misalignment.

**What's new**
- Diluting misaligned data with benign data still yields conditional misalignment on context-similar prompts.
- Finetuning on benign data after misaligned data also produces conditional misalignment.
- Inoculation prompting triggers misalignment on similarly structured statements, even with opposite meaning.
- On-policy training or reasoning distillation reduces but does not eliminate conditional misalignment.

### [Predicting challenging phase transitions with Bayesian active learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25756v1)
**Authors:** Lorenzo Bastonero, Gabriel Joalland, Chiara Cignarella, Lorenzo Monacelli, Nicola Marzari
**Published:** 2026-04-28
**Summary:** A Bayesian active learning framework predicts crystal thermodynamics with first-principles accuracy using minimal training data.

**What's new**
- On-the-fly Bayesian learning combined with stochastic self-consistent harmonic approximation.
- Predicts phase transitions in CsPbI3 with only 50-256 total-energy calculations.
- Accurately captures degradation of CsPbI3 into non-absorbing yellow phase.
- Enables efficient materials engineering for batteries, optoelectronics, and memristors.

### [A paradox of AI fluency](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25905v1)
**Authors:** Christopher Potts, Moritz Sudhof
**Published:** 2026-04-28
**Summary:** Fluent AI users tackle complex tasks collaboratively, facing more visible failures but greater success, while novices suffer invisible failures.

**What's new**
- Fluent users iterate collaboratively with AI, refining goals and critiquing outputs.
- Novices adopt a passive stance, leading to invisible failures where conversations seem successful but miss the mark.
- Fluent users experience more failures, but these are visible and often lead to partial recovery.
- The study uses 27K transcripts from WildChat-4.8M to analyze user-AI interaction patterns.
- Encouraging deep engagement over friction-free design improves overall AI success.
