# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-02-19

## What's New Today

**AI Reasoning, Safety, and Materials Simulation Advance in Cross-Domain Research**

Today's research spans from the fundamental geometry of AI alignment collapse to novel methods for simulating complex materials. Studies reveal the brittle structure of safety guardrails in fine-tuned language models and introduce interpretable, property-preserving neural operators for fluid dynamics. Meanwhile, advances in dexterous manipulation and humanoid parkour highlight progress in embodied AI.

**Highlights**
- Fine-tuning aligned LLMs on benign tasks can catastrophically break safety via geometric instability in low-dimensional subspaces.
- A new neural operator framework (Neural-POD) constructs nonlinear, orthogonal basis functions in infinite-dimensional space for physics simulation.
- Dexterous manipulation framework Dex4D learns a task-agnostic 'Anypose-to-Anypose' policy transferable from simulation to real-world tasks.
- Humanoid parkour system composes retargeted human motion skills via motion matching for agile, perception-driven navigation.
- Symbolic data-driven subgrid-scale model for fluid turbulence outperforms leading LES models without phenomenological assumptions.
- CrispEdit algorithm for LLM editing uses low-curvature projections to preserve general capabilities while changing targeted behavior.

### [The Geometry of Alignment Collapse: When Fine-Tuning Breaks Safety](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15799v1)
**Authors:** Max Springer, Chung Peng Lee, Blossom Metevier, Jane Castleman, Bohdan Turbal, Hayoung Jung, Zeyu Shen, Aleksandra Korolova
**Published:** 2026-02-17
**Summary:** Fine-tuning aligned language models on benign tasks can catastrophically degrade safety due to geometric instability in parameter space, not adversarial data or intent.

**What's new**
- Identifies structural instability: initial orthogonal updates collapse as gradient descent dynamics steer into safety-critical subspaces.
- Proves alignment concentrates in low-dimensional, high-curvature subspaces, creating brittle geometry undetectable by first-order methods.
- Formalizes Alignment Instability Condition: three geometric properties that jointly cause safety degradation via second-order acceleration.
- Establishes quartic scaling law: alignment loss grows with fourth power of training time, governed by curvature coupling.
- Reveals blind spot: current safe-fine-tuning methods address only initial snapshot, not the dynamic geometric collapse.
