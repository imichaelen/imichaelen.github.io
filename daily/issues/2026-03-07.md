### [POET-X: Memory-efficient LLM Training by Scaling Orthogonal Transformation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05500v1)
**Authors:** Zeju Qiu, Lixin Liu, Adrian Weller, Han Shi, Weiyang Liu
**Published:** 2026-03-05
**Summary:** POET-X is a memory-efficient training method for large language models that uses scalable orthogonal transformations, enabling billion-parameter model training on a single GPU.

**What's new**
- Scales the original POET framework to reduce memory use and computational cost
- Maintains training stability and generalization while boosting throughput
- Enables pretraining of billion-parameter LLMs on a single H100 GPU
- Overcomes memory limitations of standard optimizers like AdamW

### [RoboPocket: Improve Robot Policies Instantly with Your Phone](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05504v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-05

**What's new**
- Introduces Remote Inference with AR Visual Foresight to show policy trajectories on a phone, letting operators spot failures without a real robot.
- Enables Robot-Free Instant Policy Iteration, closing the learning loop in minutes via asynchronous online finetuning with incoming data.
- Doubles data efficiency over offline methods by focusing collection on policy weaknesses, adhering to scaling laws.
- Boosts sample efficiency up to 2x in distributed settings with minimal interactive corrections per person.

### [Neural Wavefunction Calculations of μSR Spectra with Quantum Muons and Protons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05453v1)
**Authors:** Jamie Carr, Mathias Volkai, W. M. C. Foulkes, Andres Perez Fadon

**Summary:** Neural quantum Monte Carlo treats muons quantum mechanically, improving hyperfine constant predictions over classical DFT for muon spin spectroscopy.

**What's new**
- Uses neural-network quantum Monte Carlo to treat muons as quantum particles in wavefunctions, not fixed classical points.

### [High-Pressure Inelastic Neutron Spectroscopy: A true test of Machine-Learned Interatomic Potential energy landscapes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05442v1)
**Authors:** Jeff Armstrong, Adam Jackson, Alin Elena
**Published:** 2026-03-05
**Summary:** High-pressure inelastic neutron spectroscopy validates a machine-learned interatomic potential's transferability across pressures for 2,5-diiodothiophene.

**What's new**
- First experimental demonstration of MLIP transferability across thermodynamic states using neutron spectroscopy.
- Uses a new high-pressure clamp cell to measure INS spectra at ambient and 1.5 GPa.

- Establishes high-pressure INS as a stringent benchmark for predictive machine-learned potentials.

### [Dissociating Direct Access from Inference in AI Introspection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05414v1)
**Authors:** Harvey Lederman, Kyle Mahowald
**Published:** 2026-03-05
**Summary:** AI models introspect via two separable mechanisms: direct, content-agnostic detection of internal anomalies and inference from prompt anomalies.

**What's new**
- Replicates thought injection detection, showing models use probability-matching and direct access to internal states.
- Direct access is content-agnostic: detects an anomaly but cannot reliably identify its semantic content.
- Models often confabulate high-frequency, concrete concepts when guessing injected content.
- Correct concept identification typically requires significantly more tokens than confabulated guesses.

### [SurvHTE-Bench: A Benchmark for Heterogeneous Treatment Effect Estimation in Survival Analysis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05483v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-05
**Summary:** SurvHTE-Bench is the first comprehensive benchmark for evaluating heterogeneous treatment effect estimation methods on right-censored survival data across synthetic, semi-synthetic, and real-world datasets.

**What's new**

- Provides semi-synthetic datasets pairing real-world covariates with simulated treatments and outcomes.
- Incorporates real-world datasets from a twin study and an HIV clinical trial with known ground truth.
- Enables rigorous comparison of survival HTE methods under diverse conditions and realistic assumption violations.

### [The Spike, the Sparse and the Sink: Anatomy of Massive Activations and Attention Sinks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05498v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-05
**Summary:** Identifies massive activations and attention sinks as distinct but architecturally linked phenomena in Transformers, with pre-norm enabling their co-occurrence.

**What's new**
- Shows massive activations create global, persistent hidden states acting as implicit model parameters.
- Demonstrates attention sinks operate locally, biasing heads toward short-range dependencies.
- Identifies pre-norm configuration as the key architectural cause for the co-occurrence of both phenomena.
- Reveals ablating pre-norm decouples the two phenomena, clarifying their distinct functional roles.
