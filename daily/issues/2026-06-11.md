## Featured Papers

### [When to Align, When to Predict: A Phase Diagram for Multimodal Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11190v1)
**Authors:** Ilay Kamai, Hugues Van Assel, Aviv Regev, Hagai B. Perets, Randall Balestriero
**Published:** 2026-06-09
**Summary:** A unified linear framework reveals when cross-modal alignment or prediction works best, producing a phase diagram with four regimes.

**What's new**
- Derives separation ratios exposing complementary failure modes of alignment and prediction.
- Introduces a phase diagram with four regimes: Both, CA only, CP only, and Neither.
- Provides a data-driven procedure to locate datasets in the diagram using a small labeled subsample.
- Validates predictions on synthetic data, stereo-vision, image-caption pairs, and astrophysical data.
- Identifies the 'Neither' regime where cross-modal training is actively harmful.

### [EEVEE: Towards Test-time Prompt Learning in the Real World for Self-Improving Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11182v1)
**Authors:** Weixian Xu, Shilong Liu, Mengdi Wang
**Published:** 2026-06-09
**Summary:** EEVEE is the first multi-dataset test-time prompt learning framework for LLM agents, improving robustness under real-world heterogeneous task streams.

**What's new**
- Introduces a router to partition heterogeneous inputs into task clusters for dedicated prompt configurations.
- Employs router-prompt co-evolution to handle mutual dependency between routing and prompt learning.
- Achieves up to 37.2% and 48.2% improvement over SOTA methods GEPA and ACE on multi-benchmark scores.

### [ABC-Bench: An Agentic Bio-Capabilities Benchmark for Biosecurity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11150v1)

**What's new**
- Introduces a benchmark for agentic biosecurity capabilities in LLMs.

### [Approaching the Limit of Intrinsic Crystalline Thermal Insulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11072v1)
**Authors:** Ruihuan Cheng, Zhiqiang Cui, Mani Jayaraman, Lincong Ji, Chen Wang, Zesheng Zeng, Petr Levinsky, Jiri Hejtmanek, Christophe Candolfi, Emmanuel Guilmeau, Xingchen Shen, Yue Chen
**Published:** 2026-06-09
**Summary:** A high-throughput workflow integrating ML potentials and phonon theory discovers crystalline materials with ultralow thermal conductivity, including record-low CsTlI4.

**What's new**
- Identified dozens of crystals with intrinsic κ below 0.2 W/m·K at room temperature.
- Reported and experimentally validated CsTlI4 with record ultralow κ of 0.14 W/m·K at 300 K.

### [It Takes One to Bias Them All: Breaking Bad with One-Shot GRPO](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10931v1)

**Published:** 2026-06-09
**Summary:** One-shot GRPO training on a single biased example can systematically break LLM alignment, inducing widespread stereotype-driven bias.

**What's new**
- One biased example via GRPO suffices to induce systematic bias across attributes and benchmarks.

- Model susceptibility varies with initial likelihood of biased outputs.

### [Attention Amnesia in Hybrid LLMs: When CoT Fine-Tuning Breaks Long-Range Recall, and How to Fix It](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11052v1)
**Authors:** Xinyu Zhou, Boyu Zhu, Yi Xu, Zhiwei Li, Yingfa Chen, Huiming Wang, Zhijiang Guo
**Published:** 2026-06-09
**Summary:** CoT fine-tuning degrades long-context recall in hybrid LLMs; QK-Restore fixes it by restoring pre-SFT query-key projections.

**What's new**
- Identifies that CoT-SFT biases attention gradients toward short-range patterns, harming long-range recall.
- Shows severe degradation: HypeNet-9B NIAH-S2@256K drops from 67.2% to 9.4% after CoT-SFT.
- Proposes QK-Restore, a training-free method restoring only W_Q and W_K from pre-SFT checkpoint.
- Introduces Procrustes variant to balance routing preservation and reasoning adaptation.
- Achieves zero-cost recovery: HypeNet-5B S3@256K improves from 65.4% to 76.4% with reasoning intact.
