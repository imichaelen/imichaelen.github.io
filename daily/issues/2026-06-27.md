### [Reinforcement Learning without Ground-Truth Solutions can Improve LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27369v1)
**Authors:** Yingyu Lin, Qiyue Gao, Nikki Lijing Kuang, Xunpeng Huang, Kun Zhou, Tongtong Liang, Zhewei Yao, Yi-An Ma, Yuxiong He
**Published:** 2026-06-25
**Summary:** RiVER trains LLMs on score-based tasks without ground-truth solutions, improving both heuristic and exact coding benchmarks.

**What's new**
- Introduces RiVER, a ranking-induced verifiable framework for RLVR without ground-truth answers.
- Identifies and addresses scale dominance and frequency dominance in continuous reward settings.
- Uses calibrated reward shaping with instance-wise comparisons and top-ranked solver emphasis.
- RiVER improves Qwen3-8B and GLM-Z1-9B-0414 by 8.9% and 9.4% in ALE rating rank.
- Transfers to exact-solution benchmarks (LiveCodeBench, USACO) with 2.4% and 3.5% average gains.

### [Autoregressive Boltzmann Generators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27361v1)
**Authors:** Danyal Rehman, Charlie B. Tan, Yoshua Bengio, Avishek Joey Bose, Alexander Tong
**Published:** 2026-06-25
**Summary:** Autoregressive Boltzmann Generators (ArBG) improve molecular sampling by replacing normalizing flows with autoregressive models, achieving better scalability and accuracy.

**What's new**
- ArBG overcomes topological constraints of normalizing flows via autoregressive modeling.
- Enables sequential inference-time interventions for enhanced flexibility.
- Outperforms flow-based models on all benchmarks, especially larger peptides like Chignolin.
- Robin model (132M params) reduces zero-shot energy error by over 60% on 8-residue systems.

### [DanceOPD: On-Policy Generative Field Distillation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27377v1)
**Authors:** Wei Zhou, Xiongwei Zhu, Zelin Xu, Bo Dong, Lixue Gong, Yongyuan Liang, Meng Chu, Leigang Qu, Lingdong Kong, Wei Liu, Tat-Seng Chua
**Published:** 2026-06-25
**Summary:** DanceOPD is an on-policy generative field distillation framework for flow-matching models that composes multiple image generation capabilities (T2I, local/global editing) without interference.

**What's new**
- Routes each sample to one capability field and queries a low-noise student-induced state for training.
- Uses a simple velocity MSE objective to learn from fields queried on the student's own rollout states.
- Absorbs operator-defined fields like classifier-free guidance into the unified framework.
- Improves multi-capability composition, strengthening target capabilities while preserving anchor generation quality.

**Published:** 2026-06-25
**Summary:** Error-conditioned Neural Solvers (ENS) use PDE residual as input to iteratively correct predictions, outperforming hybrid methods in accuracy and generalization.

### [When Does Combining Language Models Help? A Co-Failure Ceiling on Routing, Voting, and Mixture-of-Agents Across 67 Frontier Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27288v1)
**Authors:** Josef Chen
**Published:** 2026-06-25
**Summary:** Multi-model LLM systems' accuracy is capped by the co-failure rate (beta), not pairwise error correlation.

**What's new**
- Proves accuracy ceiling: any multi-model system's accuracy ≤ 1 - beta, where beta is all-models-wrong rate.
- Shows pairwise error correlation rho cannot identify beta; identical marginals and correlations can differ in all-wrong rate.
- Across 67 models, observed beta is ~2.5x higher than Gaussian copula predicts (0.052 vs 0.023 on math).
- Free-response GPQA-Diamond reopens co-failure tail (beta=0.127), showing answer format matters.
- Low-rho heterogeneous ensembles beat high-rho Self-MoA; gains come from models failing on different questions.
