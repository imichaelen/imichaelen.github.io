## Featured Papers

### [UniPool: A Globally Shared Expert Pool for Mixture-of-Experts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06665v1)
**Authors:** Minbin Huang, Han Shi, Chuanyang Zheng, Yimeng Wu, Guoxuan Chen, Xintong Yu, Yichun Yin, Hong Cheng
**Published:** 2026-05-07
**Summary:** UniPool replaces per-layer expert sets in MoE with a globally shared expert pool, reducing parameters while improving performance.

**What's new**
- Proposes UniPool, an MoE architecture with a single shared expert pool accessed by per-layer routers.
- Introduces pool-level auxiliary loss and NormRouter for stable, balanced training under sharing.
- Shows expert parameters can grow sublinearly with depth, using 41.6%-66.7% of vanilla budget to match or outperform baselines.
- Validates across five LLaMA scales (182M-978M params) on 30B tokens, reducing validation loss by up to 0.0386.

### [EMO: Pretraining Mixture of Experts for Emergent Modularity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06663v1)
**Authors:** Ryan Wang, Akshita Bhagia, Sewon Min
**Published:** 2026-05-07
**Summary:** EMO pretrains MoE models to enable modular expert use without performance loss, allowing selective deployment.

**What's new**
- EMO restricts tokens in a document to share expert pools, enabling emergent domain-specific modularity.
- Retaining only 25% of experts causes just 1% absolute performance drop, unlike standard MoEs.
- Expert subsets specialize at semantic levels (e.g., math, code) rather than low-level syntax.
- Enables memory-efficient deployment of large sparse models via composable expert subsets.

### [Why Global LLM Leaderboards Are Misleading: Small Portfolios for Heterogeneous Supervised ML](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06656v1)
**Authors:** Jai Moondra, Ayela Chughtai, Bhargavi Lanka, Swati Gupta
**Published:** 2026-05-07
**Summary:** Global LLM leaderboards are misleading due to heterogeneous opinions; small portfolios of models better capture diverse user preferences.

**What's new**
- Analyzed ~89K comparisons across 116 languages from 52 LLMs, finding global BT rankings statistically indistinguishable for top 50 models.
- Language is key: grouping by language increases ELO spread by two orders of magnitude, revealing coherent subpopulations.
- Introduced (λ, ν)-portfolios: small model sets covering ≥ν fraction of users with error ≤λ, solved via set cover with VC-dimension guarantees.
- On Arena data, 5 portfolios cover 96% of votes vs 21% by global ranking; 6 LLMs cover twice as many votes as top-6 global models.
- Applied to COMPAS dataset, portfolios detect blind spots in fairness-regularized models, aiding policymakers.
