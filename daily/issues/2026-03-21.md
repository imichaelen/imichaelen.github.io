### [NavTrust: Benchmarking Trustworthiness for Embodied Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19229v1)

**Published:** 2026-03-19
**Summary:** NavTrust is a benchmark that tests embodied navigation agents' robustness by corrupting RGB, depth, and instruction inputs in realistic scenarios.

**What's new**
- Unified benchmark for both Vision-Language and Object-Goal Navigation under realistic corruptions.
- Systematically corrupts RGB, depth, and instruction modalities to evaluate robustness gaps.

- Evaluates four mitigation strategies to improve robustness against corruptions.

### [Regret Bounds for Competitive Resource Allocation with Endogenous Costs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18999v1)
**Authors:** Rui Chai
**Published:** 2026-03-19
**Summary:** This paper analyzes online resource allocation with endogenous costs, showing a strict regret hierarchy: uniform allocation incurs linear regret, gated achieves O(T^{2/3}), and competitive allocation via multiplicative weights achieves O(sqrt(T log N)).

**What's new**
- Introduces endogenous costs in online resource allocation, where costs depend on the full allocation via an interaction matrix, unlike standard exogenous settings.
- Establishes a strict regret separation: uniform (Omega(T)), gated (O(T^{2/3})), and competitive (O(sqrt(T log N))) under adversarial sequences.
- Shows interaction topology governs a computation-regret tradeoff, with sparse graphs reducing per-step cost from O(N^2) to O(N) while increasing regret modestly.
- Identifies ring-structured topologies (e.g., Wuxing) as optimal for minimizing the product of computation and regret.
- Provides the first regret-theoretic justification for decentralized competitive allocation in modular systems with endogenous costs.
