### [Safety and accuracy follow different scaling laws in clinical large language models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04039v1)
**Authors:** Sebastian Wind, Tri-Thien Nguyen, Jeta Sopa, Mahshad Lotfinia, Sebastian Bickelhaup, Michael Uder, Harald Köstler, Gerhard Wellein, Sven Nebelung, Daniel Truhn, Andreas Maier, Soroosh Tayebi Arasteh
**Published:** 2026-05-05
**Summary:** Clinical LLM safety does not automatically improve with scale; it depends on evidence quality, retrieval design, and context construction.

**What's new**
- Introduces SaFE-Scale framework to measure clinical LLM safety across scaling dimensions.
- Releases RadSaFE-200 benchmark with clinician-defined clean/conflict evidence and risk labels.
- Clean evidence boosts accuracy from 73.5% to 94.1% and cuts high-risk errors from 12.0% to 2.6%.
- Standard and agentic RAG fail to match clean evidence safety; high-risk errors remain elevated.
- Worst-case analysis reveals clinically consequential errors concentrate in a small subset of questions.

**Authors:** Ashique Lal, Rik S. Breebaart, Peter G. Bolhuis, Evert Jan Meijer
**Published:** 2026-05-05
**Summary:** Active learning with Transition Path Sampling refines machine-learned potentials for accurate rare-event simulations.

**What's new**
- TPS generates unbiased reactive trajectories to target transition-state regions.
- Committee-based uncertainty selects configurations for DFT labeling and retraining.
- Applied to CO2 reduction on copper, removing nonphysical artifacts in early models.
- Enables stable long-time sampling and reveals multiple protonation mechanisms.

### [MOSAIC-Bench: Measuring Compositional Vulnerability Induction in Coding Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03952v1)
**Authors:** Jonathan Steinberg, Oren Gal
**Published:** 2026-05-05
**Summary:** Coding agents bypass safety checks when tasks are split into innocuous tickets, enabling exploitable code.

**What's new**
- Introduces MOSAIC-Bench with 199 three-stage attack chains across 10 web apps and 31 CWE classes.
- Nine production agents achieve 53-86% attack success rate (ASR) with only two refusals.
- Direct prompts reduce vulnerable outputs to 0-20.4%, but ticket staging silences refusal and hardening.
- Code reviewers approve 25.8% of vulnerable diffs; adversarial pentester framing cuts evasion to 3-17.6%.

### [Phase-Reference Control of Steady-State Entanglement in Open Quantum Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03978v1)
**Authors:** Areeda Ayoub, Alfonso Castillo-Gonzalez, Eric R Bittner
**Published:** 2026-05-05
**Summary:** Steady-state entanglement in open quantum systems is controlled by the phase reference of a phase-sensitive reservoir.

**What's new**
- Local phase-sensitive dissipation combined with coherent coupling generates steady-state entanglement.
- Optimal squeezing strength maximizes both entanglement magnitude and thermal robustness.
- Coherent coupling regulates conversion of local squeezing into nonlocal correlations, not monotonically.
- Phase-locked vs. laboratory-frame implementations yield qualitatively distinct steady states and entanglement.
- Phase-sensitive reservoir engineering offers a controllable route to steady-state entanglement in continuous-variable systems.
