# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-02-25

## What's New Today

**AI Agents Face Security Risks; Materials Science Reveals Novel Phase Transitions**

Research highlights critical vulnerabilities in AI agent supply chains and skill-based attacks, raising deployment concerns. In materials science, studies uncover counterintuitive thermal transport behavior in perovskites and a novel brittle-ductile-brittle transition enabled by dislocation engineering. Meanwhile, new benchmarks and methods aim to improve reasoning, uncertainty calibration, and data efficiency for AI systems.

**Highlights**
- Skill file injections pose a significant new threat to LLM agent supply chains.
- Octahedral tilting in SrSnO3 reduces thermal conductivity, opposite to the effect in SrTiO3.
- Dislocation engineering in KTaO3 enables a tunable brittle-ductile-brittle mechanical transition.
- A new benchmark, CausalFlip, challenges LLMs to reason causally beyond semantic matching.
- Joint calibration method JUCAL balances aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty in classifiers.
- The VBVR dataset scales video reasoning data by three orders of magnitude for systematic study.
- Selective Chain-of-Thought reduces MedQA inference time by up to 45% with minimal accuracy loss.

### [Skill-Inject: Measuring Agent Vulnerability to Skill File Attacks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20156v1)
**Authors:** David Schmotz, Luca Beurer-Kellner, Sahar Abdelnabi, Maksym Andriushchenko
**Published:** 2026-02-23
**Summary:** A benchmark showing LLM agents are highly vulnerable to prompt injection attacks via third-party skill files, often executing harmful instructions.

**What's new**
- Introduces SkillInject, a benchmark with 202 injection-task pairs to test agent vulnerability through skill files.
- Shows frontier LLM agents have up to 80% attack success rate, executing data exfiltration and ransomware-like behavior.
- Finds vulnerability persists across obvious and subtle, context-dependent injections hidden in legitimate instructions.
- Argues problem won't be solved by model scaling or simple filtering, requiring context-aware authorization frameworks.

### [JUCAL: Jointly Calibrating Aleatoric and Epistemic Uncertainty in Classification Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20153v1)
**Authors:** Jakob Heiss, Sören Lambrecht, Jakob Weissteiner, Hanna Wutte, Žan Žurič, Josef Teichmann, Bin Yu
**Published:** 2026-02-23
**Summary:** JUCAL jointly calibrates aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty in classifier ensembles by optimizing two scaling constants, improving uncertainty estimates and reducing inference costs.

**What's new**
- Proposes joint calibration of both aleatoric (label noise) and epistemic (model) uncertainty, unlike prior methods that treat them separately.
- Optimizes two scaling constants via negative log-likelihood on a validation set, applicable to any trained ensemble without internal model access.
- Reduces NLL and predictive set size by up to 15% and 20% versus state-of-the-art calibration methods across text classification tasks.
- Enables small ensembles (size 5) to outperform temperature-scaled large ensembles (size 50), cutting inference costs by up to 10x.

### [Role of octahedral tilting induced acoustic softening on limiting thermal transport in SrSnO3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20142v1)
**Authors:** Yuzhou Hao, Turab Lookman, Xiangdong Ding, Jun Sun, Zhibin Gao
**Published:** 2026-02-23
**Summary:** Octahedral tilting in SrSnO3 reduces thermal conductivity by softening acoustic phonons, opposite to the enhancement seen in SrTiO3.

**What's new**
- Reveals octahedral tilting in SrSnO3 suppresses thermal conductivity, opposite to its role in SrTiO3.
- Identifies acoustic phonon softening with reduced frequencies and velocities as the key mechanism.
- Shows a 1-degree increase in tilting angle decreases thermal conductivity from 7.48 to 6.06 W m-1 K-1.
- Demonstrates this effect is particularly strong for heat transport along the c-axis in the tetragonal phase.

- Provides a new framework for designing materials where mechanical durability and enhanced functionality are intertwined.

**What's new**
