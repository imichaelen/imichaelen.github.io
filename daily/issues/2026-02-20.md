## What's New Today

**AI Agents, Materials Simulation, and LLM Reliability Dominate Research Landscape**

Research advances span the robustness of AI agents, the scalability of physics simulations, and the interpretability of large language models. New frameworks for policy enforcement and agent reliability aim to make AI systems more dependable, while breakthroughs in quantum Monte Carlo and dislocation theory push the boundaries of materials modeling. Concurrently, studies probe the limits of LLM generalization, safety alignment, and their practical utility in scientific and educational settings.

- PCAS introduces a policy compiler with deterministic enforcement for secure LLM-based agentic systems.

- A study finds LLM assistance shows no significant improvement over internet search for novice biology task performance.
- New benchmark defines 12 concrete metrics to holistically evaluate AI agent reliability beyond accuracy.
- Causal inference framework clarifies what interpretability studies on LLMs can and cannot justify.
- Universal ratio E_loop/(k_B T_m) ≈ 25.1 derived as a geometric constant for dislocation-loop mediated melting.

## Featured Papers

### [Policy Compiler for Secure Agentic Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16708v1)
**Authors:** Nils Palumbo, Sarthak Choudhary, Jihye Choi, Prasad Chalasani, Mihai Christodorescu, Somesh Jha
**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** PCAS is a policy compiler that enforces security policies in LLM-based agent systems by modeling state as a dependency graph and using declarative rules for deterministic enforcement.

**What's new**
- Models agent system state as a dependency graph to track causal relationships and information flow across agents
- Uses a Datalog-derived language for declarative policies that account for transitive information flow and cross-agent provenance
- Provides deterministic policy enforcement via a reference monitor that blocks violations before execution
- Compiles existing agent implementations into policy-compliant systems without requiring security-specific restructuring
- Demonstrates improved policy compliance from 48% to 93% in customer service tasks with zero violations in instrumented runs

### [Reinforced Fast Weights with Next-Sequence Prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16704v1)
**Authors:** Hee Seung Hwang, Xindi Wu, Sanghyuk Chun, Olga Russakovsky
**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** REFINE trains fast weight models with next-sequence prediction via RL, improving long-context performance over next-token training.

**What's new**
- Uses next-sequence prediction (NSP) via RL to capture multi-token coherence, unlike next-token prediction (NTP).
- Selects high-entropy token positions for rollouts and assigns self-supervised sequence-level rewards.
- Optimizes with group relative policy optimization (GRPO) for stable training.
- Applicable mid-training, post-training, or at test-time for pre-trained language models.
- Demonstrates gains in retrieval, QA, and LongBench tasks for models like LaCT-760M and DeltaNet-1.3B.

### [Addressing Ill-conditioning in Density Functional Theory for Reliable Machine Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16618v1)
**Authors:** L. Arnstein, J. Wetherell, R. Lawrence, P. J. Hasnip, M. J. P. Hodgson
**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** The paper identifies two classes of ill-conditioned density functionals in DFT that hinder ML accuracy and shows using the external potential as input improves predictions.

**What's new**
- Identifies two classes of ill-conditioned density functionals: globally gauge-dependent quantities and those implicitly dependent on (N+1)-electron density.
- Shows ML models have orders-of-magnitude greater error for these ill-conditioned functionals compared to well-conditioned ones.
- Demonstrates that providing the external potential as input to ML models significantly improves predictions for these problematic quantities.

### [Protecting the Undeleted in Machine Unlearning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16697v1)

### [Ab Initio Auxiliary-Field Quantum Monte Carlo in the Thermodynamic Limit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16679v1)
**Authors:** Jinghong Zhang, Meng-Fu Chen, Adam Rettig, Tong Jiang, Paul J. Robinson, Hieu Q. Dinh, Anton Z. Ni, Joonho Lee
**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** A new AFQMC method achieves O(N³) scaling for solids, enabling direct thermodynamic-limit and complete-basis-set calculations for accurate energies and magnetic observables.

**What's new**
- Combines tensor hypercontraction and k-point symmetry to reduce AFQMC scaling to O(N³) computational and O(N²) memory.
- Enables direct thermodynamic-limit and complete-basis-set calculations without empirical corrections or embedding schemes.
- Provides a general-purpose, systematically improvable framework for predictive simulations of insulating, metallic, and correlated solids.

### [Measuring Mid-2025 LLM-Assistance on Novice Performance in Biology](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16703v1)

**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** A 2025 randomized trial found LLM assistance did not significantly improve novice completion of a lab workflow but was associated with modest performance benefits in some tasks.

**What's new**
- Found no significant difference in overall workflow completion between LLM-assisted and internet-only novice groups.
- Observed a notable but non-significant success increase for a cell culture task with LLM assistance.

- Highlights a gap between AI benchmark performance and real-world utility in laboratory settings.

### [Causality is Key for Interpretability Claims to Generalise](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16698v1)
**Authors:** Shruti Joshi, Aaron Mueller, David Klindt, Wieland Brendel, Patrik Reizinger, Dhanya Sridhar
**Published:** 2026-02-18
**Summary:** The paper argues that causal inference, particularly Pearl's hierarchy, is essential for making valid and generalizable interpretability claims about LLMs, distinguishing associations, interventions, and counterfactuals.

**What's new**
- Applies Pearl's causal hierarchy to LLM interpretability, clarifying what evidence supports associations, interventions, or counterfactuals.
- Highlights that counterfactual claims (e.g., what output would have been under unobserved edits) are largely unverifiable without controlled supervision.
- Shows how causal representation learning operationalizes this hierarchy, specifying recoverable variables from activations and required assumptions.
- Proposes a diagnostic framework to help practitioners match interpretability methods and evaluations to the strength of claims for generalizable findings.
