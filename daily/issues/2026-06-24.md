### [AutoDex: An Automated Real-World System for Dexterous Grasping Data Collection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23689v1)
**Authors:** Mingi Choi, Gunhee Kim, Jisoo Kim, Taeksoo Kim, Taeyun Ha, Jongbin Lim, Hanbyul Joo
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** AutoDex automates real-world dexterous grasping data collection with a closed-loop system, achieving 4.8x faster throughput than teleoperation.

**What's new**
- Fully automated loop: perception, execution, labeling, and reset without human intervention.
- Dense 20-camera setup overcomes severe hand-object occlusion for accurate object localization.
- Active object reset exposes diverse stable poses, enriching the grasp database.
- Collected 3,593 trials across two robot hands on 100 objects with synchronized multi-view data.
- Retrieved grasps from AutoDex-validated database succeed 76% vs. 34% for simulation-only validation.

### [Randomized YaRN Improves Length Generalization for Long-Context Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23687v1)
**Authors:** Manas Mehta, Fangcong Yin, Greg Durrett
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** Randomized YaRN improves LLM length generalization by combining YaRN extrapolation with randomized positional encoding and a length curriculum.

**What's new**
- Proposes Randomized YaRN, a training method that exposes models to out-of-distribution positional representations on short inputs.
- Combines YaRN-based positional extrapolation with randomized positional encoding and a length curriculum.
- Evaluated on BABILong and MRCR benchmarks, showing consistent gains on context lengths from 16K to 128K.
- Outperforms standard fine-tuning, with largest improvements at far out-of-distribution lengths.

### [CoorDex: Coordinating Body and Hand Priors for Continuous Dexterous Humanoid Loco-Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23680v1)
**Authors:** Sikai Li, Shuning Li, Zhenyu Wei, Yunchao Yao, Chenran Li, Mingyu Ding
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** CoorDex enables continuous dexterous loco-manipulation for humanoids by coordinating body and hand priors via latent residual control.

**What's new**
- Converts high-dimensional body and dexterous hand control into coordinated latent residual actions.
- Trains privileged motion tracking teachers and distills them into proprioception-conditioned latent priors.
- Uses frozen priors as action space for downstream residual RL with separate body-hand heads.
- Demonstrates non-stop bottle grasping, fridge door opening, and cube pick-and-turn on a Unitree G1 with 20-DoF hand.
- Outperforms joint-space PPO, joint-space hand control, and monolithic latent prediction in walk-grasp-carry tasks.

### [Tapered Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23670v1)
**Authors:** Reza Bayat, Ali Behrouz, Aaron Courville
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** Tapered Language Models improve perplexity by allocating more capacity to earlier layers and less to later layers under a fixed budget.

**What's new**
- Shows that uniform layer capacity is suboptimal; earlier layers benefit from more parameters.
- Introduces TLMs: monotonically tapering MLP width across depth via a cosine schedule.
- Consistently improves perplexity and downstream tasks across 4 architectures at no extra cost.
- Demonstrates depth-aware capacity allocation as a simple, architecture-agnostic design lever.

### [SVD-Surgeon: Optimal Singular-Value Surgery for Large Language Model Compression](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23568v1)

### [Helical Domain-Wall-Ring Networks Reshape Superconducting Correlations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23599v1)
**Authors:** Yi-Chun Hung, Arun Bansil
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** Helical domain-wall-ring networks in moiré materials reshape superconducting correlations via confinement effects.

**What's new**
- Inter-ring SC-pair tunneling is renormalization-group relevant, enhancing correlations through phase locking.
- Finite-size effects strongly suppress phase-locking scale even when infinite-size theory predicts strong coupling.
- SC scaling dimension decreases with twist angle, insensitive to pair-tunneling strength, diverging from infinite-size predictions.
- Ring networks exhibit qualitatively distinct collective behavior due to confinement and geometry interplay.

### [Can LLMs Reliably Self-Report Adversarial Prefills, and How?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23671v1)
**Authors:** Quang Minh Nguyen, Uzair Ahmed, Taegyoon Kim
**Published:** 2026-06-22
**Summary:** LLMs cannot reliably recognize when their outputs are caused by adversarial prefill attacks, with introspective signals being fragile and probe-dependent.

**What's new**
- No model reliably recognizes its own compromised outputs; average claiming rate on prefilled responses is 27.3%.
- Introspective signal stems from safety/refusal reasoning; orthogonalizing weights against refusal direction collapses the gap.
- Signal is probe-dependent: framing as internal intention vs. external tampering yields different responses on same models.
- LoRA finetuning (SFT, GRPO, DPO) widens intention-probe gap on 8B-27B models but does not transfer to tampering probe.
- Intervention counterintuitively raises attack success rate under adversarial prefill on most models, offering only partial mitigation.
