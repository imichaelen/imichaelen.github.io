# Daily Issue (JST): 2026-02-12

## What's New Today

**AI Reasoning Efficiency, Safety, and Materials Modeling Advance in Daily Research**

Today's research spans efficient reasoning for large language models, with new methods for compressing chains of thought and detecting internal biases. In materials science, automated frameworks for predicting vibrational spectra and modeling complex polymer degradation emerge. Cross-cutting themes include test-time scaling, uncertainty quantification, and the interplay between geometric structure and model behavior.

**Highlights**
- New frameworks compress verbose Chain-of-Thought reasoning in LLMs to reduce computational cost without sacrificing accuracy.
- Automated black-box pipeline detects 'unverbalized biases' where LLM reasoning traces hide internal model biases.
- Physics-informed neural networks and universal machine learning potentials enable efficient prediction of material properties.
- Geometric interference identified as root cause for diffusion model failure on representation encoders, solved via Riemannian flow matching.
- Step-resolved data attribution method reveals how individual training examples influence specific steps in looped transformer reasoning.
- Conformal prediction algorithm provides statistical guarantees for uncertainty in instance segmentation masks.

### [Biases in the Blind Spot: Detecting What LLMs Fail to Mention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10117v1)
**Authors:** Iván Arcuschin, David Chanin, Adrià Garriga-Alonso, Oana-Maria Camburu
**Published:** 2026-02-10
**Summary:** An automated pipeline detects biases that LLMs rely on but do not mention in their reasoning, revealing hidden influences in decision tasks.

**What's new**
- Introduces unverbalized biases: biases LLMs use but omit from chain-of-thought reasoning.
- Fully automated, black-box pipeline generates and tests bias concepts without predefined categories.
- Uses statistical methods for multiple testing and early stopping to validate biases efficiently.
- Discovers new biases like Spanish fluency and writing formality in hiring, loans, and admissions tasks.
- Automatically confirms known biases (e.g., gender, race) while uncovering previously unknown ones.

### [Theory for enzymatic degradation of semi-crystalline polymer particles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10087v1)
**Authors:** Michael Schindler, Hernan Garate, Ludwik Leibler
**Published:** 2026-02-10
**Summary:** A geometric model explains how growing crystalline spherulites compete with surface erosion during enzymatic degradation of semi-crystalline plastics like PET.

**What's new**
- Presents a geometric model for enzymatic degradation where crystal growth competes with surface erosion.
- Introduces a new extension of Voronoi/Delaunay tessellation in space to solve the model numerically.
- Model parameters are extracted from experimental data on PET degradation by hydrolase LCC-ICCG.
- Correctly predicts both final degradation yield and degradation kinetics for PET waste.
- Clarifies how nucleating agents or impurities can undermine pretreatment efforts for low-crystallinity materials.
