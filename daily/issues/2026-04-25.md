### [The Sample Complexity of Multicalibration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21923v1)
**Authors:** Natalie Collina, Jiuyao Lu, Georgy Noarov, Aaron Roth
**Published:** 2026-04-23
**Summary:** Multicalibration sample complexity is Θ̃(ε^{-3}) for |G| ≤ ε^{-κ}, separating from marginal calibration's Θ̃(ε^{-2}).

**What's new**
- Proves minimax sample complexity of multicalibration is Θ̃(ε^{-3}) for group families up to size ε^{-κ}.
- Shows a sharp threshold: at κ=0, complexity drops to Θ̃(ε^{-2}), matching marginal calibration.
- Establishes matching bounds for weighted L_p multicalibration with optimal exponent 3/p for 1≤p≤2.
- Extends lower bounds to a regular class of elicitable properties, including expectiles and quantiles.

### [Supervised Learning Has a Necessary Geometric Blind Spot: Theory, Consequences, and Minimal Repair](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21395v1)
**Authors:** Vishal Rajput
**Published:** 2026-04-23
**Summary:** Supervised learning has a necessary geometric blind spot: encoders must retain sensitivity to label-correlated but nuisance directions, proven mathematically and repairable via PMH.

**What's new**
- Proves Theorem 1: ERM forces non-zero Jacobian sensitivity in label-correlated nuisance directions, a necessary geometric blind spot.
- Unifies four empirical phenomena: non-robust features, texture bias, corruption fragility, and robustness-accuracy tradeoff under one theory.
- Introduces Trajectory Deviation Index (TDI) to measure isotropic path-length distortion, detecting failure modes missed by alternatives.
- Shows blind spot worsens with model scale (66M to 340M) and ERM fine-tuning (+54%), but PMH repairs it by 11x with a unique Gaussian perturbation term.
